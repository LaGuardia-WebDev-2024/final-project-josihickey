Nanny's Giuggiuleni Rolls

These are my Nanny's (my great-grandmother) rolls! I think I'd give nearly anything to have one again. She used to make them for a Sunday night dinner. It was always very special when she made them.They were the absolute best. I wrote this down using the recipe on the index card she wrote, but it's not very specific, and there's no instructions, so I put it together where I could. I've only made them once, so bare with me.

  • Time: About 1.5 hours
  • Serves: Probably 12
Ingredients Quantity
Rapid Yeast 1 pkg.
Flour 5 cups
Salt 1/2 tbs
Hot Water 1 cup
Crisco (vegetable oil) 1/4 cup
Giuggiuleni (sesame seeds) For on top
Egg (optional) 1 egg

Instructions

I will say that the ingredients list written by my Nanny and the ingredient quantity in the steps are slightly different, but I'm using my Nanny's index card. I will say to go a bot heavier on the salt than she did though. The written instructions say something more along the ines of one heaping tbs.

  1. Mix flour, instant yeast (1 packet), and 1 (heaping) tbs salt
  2. Add crisco and hot water until soft, sticky, and a little wet. Knead for 10 min.
  3. Let rise for 10 minutes
  4. Grease baking sheet or pan.
  5. Shape rolls on floured surface; put on sheet. Cover with a towel and let rise for an hour.
  6. Beat one egg and brush on top. Sprinle giuggiuleni (sesame seeds) on top.
  7. Bake at 350 for 45 minutes.

Succo

Another one of my Nanny's recipies! Unless it was her mother's, I'm not sure. It's essentially a Sicilian-American Sunday sauce slowly cooked with meat in it. My mom (or maybe my Nana) used to tell me that Nanny would start making it early Sunday morning, go to church, let it cook all day, and then they would have dinner around 4. While it does take all day, I promise it is so worth it! I'm also vegetarian, so I usually use other protien options instead of the meat, so if you do that just add less sugar.

  • Time: All day, but in a low energy way. Making the sauce doesn't take too long.
  • Serves: You'll be eating it the rest of the week!
Ingredients Quantity
Water 2/3 of the yellow pot (like a medium but wide pot)
Tomato Paste 3 tiny cans
Tomato Puree (Crushed tomatoes?) 1 huge can
Sugar 1 cup (use less if you don't like sweet sauces)
Garlic To taste
Onion Salt To taste
Italian Seasoning To taste
Meat (usually Italian sausage and Meatballs) Your call

Instructions

  1. Fill the yellow pot 2/3 with water.
  2. Add 3 tiny cans of tomato paste and 1 huge can of tomato puree. Stir in all the tomato paste so it doesn't stick to the bottom (or you'll get smoku)
  3. Add 1 cup sugar and then diminishing amounts of garlic and onion salt and italian seasoning to taste.
  4. Bring to a boil and reduce to a simmer.
  5. Add in meat and cook forever--and as my mom sent me--on low for hours.
  6. Serve over your favorite pasta and enjoy!

Grandma Helen's Stuffing

Ingredients Quantity
White Bread 2 loaves
Butter A few large spoonfuls
Onion A lot (maybe 4-6?)
Celery A Pack
Seasonings:
  • Celery Salt
  • Garlic Salt
  • Onion Salt
  • Sage
  • Mrs. Dash (without salt)
  • Pepper
  • *Optional: A touch of white pepper
 To taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the celery and onion into small pieces.
  2. In a largre pan (or two), heat up the butter. Add celery and onion and stir. Cook until slightly soft and brown. Lightly season with salt and pepper.
  3. Get a big pot ready. Take one loaf of bread and wet or two or three pieces at a time. They should be slightly wet, but not soaked. Then, tear the bread and pt in a the pot in small (but not tiny) pieces.
  4. Add some of the cooked celery and onion to the pot with the bread and stir together. Then, add the seasonings to taste. Stir to combine. *Hint: Add very little sage, and balance which flavor of salt you add. Taste intermittedly to figure out what you need! also, you need less pepper than you think.
  5. Repeat steps three and four until all the bread and celer-onion mixture is combined, and season to your liking.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350 F. Put the stuffing into a baking dish (or two) and spread evenly. Bake in the oven until fully cooked. Remove from the oven, and let cool.
  7. Enjoy!

Lemon and Parmesan Pasta

This is a recipe of my own, adapted from the cookbook Sicilia by Ben Tish. It makes for a great late night meal, and is pretty easy to throw together. It's essentially my quicker version for when I don't have the energy to cook. By the way, you can totally make it with leftover pasta, I do all the time.

  • Time:It's a race. Maybe 10-15, not counting the time it takes to cook the pasta.
  • Serves: As much as you want.
Ingredients Quantity
Pasta of your choice (I reccomend something like Sagnarelli As much as you want to eat
Olive Oil A drizzle, added as needed
Breadcrumbs A little sprinkle (as much as you want, but don't go to heavy)
Garlic A spoon (as much as you want)
Lemon Juice A decent amount. Depends on how lemony you want it.
Parmesan, grated A hefty amount
Salt, pepper, red pepper To taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta and set aside. Save some of the pasta water for later.
  2. In a medium sized pan, start heating up some olive oil. Then, add the breadcrumbs. In an ideal world, you would lightly toast it. If you're in a rush, cook it until your patience runs out because you're too hungry.
  3. Add garlic. Then add in cooked pasta and lemon juice. Cook for like a minute or so.
  4. Remove the pan from heat. Add in a splash on the reserved pasta water. Start mixing in the cheese, a bit at a time, until you're happy with it.
  5. Add any additional seasonings you'd like. Enjoy!

Pastina

This is a fabulous meal if you are feeling a bit under the weather, or just want something nice and warm and hearty! Also, this probably isn't the traditional way to make it, but it's the way I usually make it because I don't know that way.

  • Time:30 minutes? I never checked.
  • Serves: About one decently large bowl
Ingredients Quantity
Pastina 1/4 Cup
Celery Two stalks
White Onion 1/2 an onion
Garlic As much as you want
Water 2 cups
Vegetable (or your choice of flavour) Buillon
**Alternative:substitute water and buillon with 2 cups broth of your choice 		2 tbs
Butter (optional, can also be substituted with olive oil) Like a spoon or two
Parmesan, grated The more the merrier!
Salt, pepper, red pepper (optional) To taste!

Instructions

  1. Chop the celery and onion (and the garlic if you're using fresh garlic. I never do). Dice the onion and cut the celery in the normal way to cut it.
  2. In a medium sized pot, melt a bit of butter, and throw in the garlic. Cook that for a tiny bit, and then add the celery and onion. Cook until it looks good (you don't need to fully cook it because it will cook in the broth).
  3. Add two cups of water to the pot, and bring to a boil.
  4. When the water boils, stir in the buillon. If you just used broth to start out with, skip this step.
  5. Add in the pastina. Bring down the heat a bit, and cover
  6. Cook the pastina until the liquid has been largely absorbed, about 6-8 minutes. Stir when you remember to.
  7. Remove the pot from heat. Stir in parmesan, and any additional butter or olive oil you wanted to add. Then, season to taste.
  8. Serve in a bowl, and add a little parmesan on top! Enjoy!
